Key Brands

Balenciaga is one of the most influential fashion houses, known for its avant-garde approach and deconstruction of classic silhouettes. Under the direction of Demna Gvasalia, the brand has become a symbol of postmodern fashion, blending luxury with streetwear aesthetics. Its iconic Triple S sneakers, exaggerated proportions, and dystopian themes have solidified Balenciaga’s status as a leader in contemporary design.

Vivienne Westwood is a pioneer of punk fashion, breaking traditional fashion norms since the 1970s. She infused rebellious, anarchic elements into classic British tailoring, making her brand a symbol of countercultural expression. Her signature corsets, asymmetrical cuts, and politically charged designs transformed the fashion landscape, merging activism with style.

Maison Margiela is renowned for its conceptual approach to fashion and innovative garment deconstruction. Founded by Martin Margiela, the brand is known for anonymity, unconventional tailoring, and an experimental use of materials. The label’s signature Tabi boots, reconstructed vintage pieces, and minimalist aesthetic redefine modern luxury through a lens of artistic expression

Dior represents the epitome of elegance and timeless sophistication. Founded by Christian Dior in 1946, the brand revolutionized post-war fashion with the "New Look," emphasizing femininity through structured silhouettes and luxurious fabrics. Over the years, Dior has continued to evolve, seamlessly blending classic haute couture with modern influences, making it one of the most prestigious names in high fashion.
