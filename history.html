History of Archive Fashion

♪ Music ♪

Exploring the evolution of iconic archive fashion brands.

Number (N)ine

Founded in 1997 by Takahiro Miyashita, Number (N)ine became a cult brand that blended punk aesthetics, grunge, and high fashion influences. Known for its deconstructed tailoring, layered silhouettes, and musical references—especially to rock bands like Nirvana—the brand gained recognition in the early 2000s. One of its most iconic collections, "Give Peace a Chance" (2004), showcased military-inspired designs with a rebellious, anti-war message. The brand’s distressed denim, asymmetrical cuts, and dark, moody themes left a lasting impact on Japanese and Western streetwear. Despite Miyashita leaving the brand in 2009, Number (N)ine's legacy continues to influence modern menswear and underground fashion scenes.

Dior

Dior has played a crucial role in shaping the landscape of archive fashion, leaving a lasting impact on the industry since its foundation in 1946 by Christian Dior. The brand revolutionized post-war fashion with the introduction of the “New Look” in 1947, which emphasized femininity through cinched waists, voluminous skirts, and elegant silhouettes. This marked a shift from the austere wartime styles and redefined luxury fashion.

Under Hedi Slimane’s direction in Dior Homme (2000–2007), the brand became synonymous with the skinny silhouette, which influenced a new era of menswear. His tailored, rock-inspired aesthetic has since become a key reference in archival fashion, with pieces from his collections fetching high prices on the resale market.

Dior’s influence extends beyond clothing, as the brand has redefined luxury branding, exclusivity, and craftsmanship. Vintage Dior pieces, particularly from Galliano and Slimane’s eras, remain highly coveted in the world of archive fashion, serving as a testament to the house’s lasting cultural and artistic impact.

Rick Owens

Rick Owens, often called the “Lord of Darkness” in fashion, launched his eponymous brand in 1994, redefining luxury with an avant-garde and gothic approach. His designs are characterized by asymmetry, drapery, distressed leather, and oversized silhouettes, creating a unique balance between elegance and dystopian aesthetics. Owens’ Geobasket sneakers, Stooges leather jackets, and longline tunics became fashion staples, blending high-end craftsmanship with rebellious energy. His collaborations with Adidas, Birkenstock, and Moncler brought his aesthetic to a wider audience, while his brutalist runway shows continue to challenge conventional fashion norms. More than just a designer, Rick Owens represents an uncompromising artistic vision that pushes the boundaries of contemporary fashion.

Other Influential Brands

  • Helmut Lang – Known for minimalism and innovation in textiles.
  • Maison Margiela – The pioneer of deconstructionism and anonymity.
  • Yohji Yamamoto – Master of oversized silhouettes and artistic tailoring.
  • Undercover – Jun Takahashi’s brand mixing punk, streetwear, and conceptual design.

Did You Know?

Many archive fashion pieces gain value over time, with some rare garments selling for tens of thousands of dollars on the resale market.
