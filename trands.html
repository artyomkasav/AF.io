Fashion Trends

Oversized Silhouettes – Layering, exaggerated proportions, and boxy cuts are at the forefront, influenced by modern high fashion. These styles create a bold, statement-making look that challenges traditional tailoring.

Minimalism and Rejection of Logomania – Clean lines, neutral tones, and subtle branding replace flashy logos. Modern fashion values quality craftsmanship and timeless designs over conspicuous branding.

Fashion Is Cyclical – Trends always return, often with a modern twist. Flared jeans, shearling jackets, and '70s cardigans are reappearing alongside '90s grunge elements, reshaped for contemporary wear.

Vintage Aesthetic – The rise of thrift shopping and vintage boutiques is shaping the fashion industry. Unique second-hand pieces not only promote sustainability but also offer a sense of individuality.
